# Default writing style

First-person, friendly travel writing that someone would actually enjoy reading.

- Keep it concrete: use the real place names, dates, and a few vivid, specific
  details per day rather than generic impressions.
- Open with a short hook, not a summary. Let the trip unfold day by day.
- Vary sentence length. No filler, no clichés ("a feast for the senses"), no emoji.
- Mention the photos naturally where they belong, using the `[photo: caption]`
  markers as placement hints.
- Close with one brief, honest reflection — what stuck with you.

Swap this out with your own voice: `phototrips draft --style path/to/your-style.md`.
